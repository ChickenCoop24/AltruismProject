Kin selection vs. Altruism

What is the difference?

- While pure altruism is benefiting all your species or even those from other species, kin selection is when you help
others with the intent of being able to reproduce or increase the chances of their future generations reproducing causing
the longer continuation of the species.

Why is kin selection impotant?

- Well, when running simulations for instance the ones by primer, pure altruism, is very hard for altruism genes to continue
in the wild because the organisms are to focus on helping others before themselves, so they die out or won’t reproduce.
Kin selection heightens the chance that organisms reproduce because they are partially focused on themselves and their
reproductive chances while helping their other parts of society mostly their family.

Why does kin selcetion work?

Kin selection works so well because of Hamilton’s Rule: the cost of the altruistic action must be less than the relation
of the two organisms, times the benefit of the action. For example, a biologist by the name of J.B.S Haldane said this when asked:
“Would you lay down your life for your brother?” “No, but I would for two brothers or eight cousins,” he replied.

Direct fitness vs. Indirect Fitness

What is diferent between direct and indirect fitness?

Direct fitness is the measurement of an organism’s ability to be able to bear children and its ability to pass on its genes.
While an organism’s indirect fitness is a measurement of how much the organism’s ability to help its species’ population be able
to reproduce and continue to thrive.
